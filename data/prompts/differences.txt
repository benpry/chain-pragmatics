###
"He always had an interest for the fruit of knowledge"
Which of these is an accurate paraphrase of the above sentence?
a) He always had an interest for the benefits of knowledge
b) He always had an interest for the origins of knowledge
c) He always had an interest for the pineapple of knowledge
d) He always had an interest for the ways knowledge can be practically used

The answer is a).
Fruit is a type of food that is sweet and nourishing. Knowledge is an abstract concept that people cannot eat. This metaphor implies that knowledge conveys benefits like eating fruit. Therefore, the most appropriate paraphrase is "He always had an interest for the benefits of knowledge"

"The darted harpoon was lightning in the sea."
Which of these is an accurate paraphrase of the above sentence?
a) The darted harpoon was slow and grey in the sea
b) The harpoon was darted in the sea.
c) The darted harpoon was quick and shiny in the sea.
d) The harpoon was in the sea and there was a lightning flash.

The answer is c).
Lightning is fast and bright. A harpoon is normally stationary and can be shiny. This metaphor implies that the harpoon was fast and bright. Therefore, the most appropriate paraphrase is "The darted harpoon was quick and shiny in the sea."

"I had already planted the idea in her mind."
Which of these is an accurate paraphrase of the above sentence?
a) I had already persuaded her of the idea.
b) I had already insinuated the idea to her.
c) I had already planted the seed when an idea crossed my mind
d) I had already scared her about the idea.

The answer is b).
Planting is the process of putting a plant in the ground so it can grow. Ideas are not plants as they are abstract concepts and minds cannot literally grow things. This metaphor implies that I had put the idea in her mind so that it could grow. Therefore, the most appropriate paraphrase is "I had already insinuated the idea to her."

"The faculty meeting was a war"
Which of these is an accurate paraphrase of the above sentence?
a) At the faculty meeting we talked about war
b) The faculty meeting was very difficult and emotional
c) The faculty meeting was ridiculous
d) The faculty meeting was highly aggressive

The answer is d).
Wars are very aggressive and violent events in which people fight and die. Faculty meetings are usually not physically violent like wars, but people can be aggressive in them. This metaphor implies that the faculty meeting was aggressive like a war could be. Therefore, the most appropriate paraphrase is "The faculty meeting was highly aggressive"

"The weeks crawled by until we could meet again"
Which of these is an accurate paraphrase of the above sentence?
a) The weeks seemed to pass slowly until we could meet again
b) the weeks seemed to pass fast until we could meet again
c) the weeks went as usual until we could meet again
d) during the week, people crawled by until we could meet again

The answer is a).
Crawling is an action that involves slowly moving on ones hands and knees. Weeks are and divisions of time and cannot move. This metaphor implies that the weeks passed slowly. Therefore, the most appropriate paraphrase is "The weeks seemed to pass slowly until we could meet again."
